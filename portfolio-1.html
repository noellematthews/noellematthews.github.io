Crash Safety in Portland

A capstone project exploring how infrastructure affects crash risk for cyclists and pedestrians.

This project is the culmination of my MS in Data Science program. Alongside my partner Thomas Sato, I’m investigating how Portland’s street-level features—like greenways, lighting, and traffic calming devices—relate to the frequency and severity of crashes involving bikes and pedestrians.

We're working with spatial data from 2007–2022, cleaning and aligning multiple city datasets to support intersection-level analysis. Our pipeline includes ingestion, transformation, schema normalization, exploratory analysis, and predictive modeling.

We’ve built a unified schema centered on a normalized intersections table, connecting crash records to layers like bike routes, street lighting, traffic volumes, and calming features. Early insights are already revealing where gaps in infrastructure may correspond with higher injury risk.

This is a live project that will be presented on August 12, 2025. The PDF linked below gives a detailed look at our data sources, architecture, schema, and early findings.
