Crash Safety in Portland

A data-driven look at infrastructure and crash risk for cyclists and pedestrians.

This project is currently in progress as part of my MSDS capstone and will be presented on August 12, 2025. Alongside my partner Thomas Sato, I’m analyzing how street-level infrastructure—like neighborhood greenways, lighting, and traffic calming features—relates to the frequency and severity of bicycle- and pedestrian-involved crashes across Portland from 2007–2022.

We’re currently in the exploratory and modeling phase, having completed data ingestion and schema design. Our biggest challenge so far has been harmonizing diverse, spatially misaligned public datasets into a normalized structure that supports intersection-level analysis. To guide that effort, we consulted with city planners and Vision Zero analysts.

The project uses a central intersections table to unify crash records, lighting data, recommended bike routes, traffic calming features, and volume counts. Our early visualizations already suggest meaningful patterns between infrastructure features and crash outcomes.
